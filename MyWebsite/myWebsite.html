I grew in Kansas City MO, each day I fall more in love with the city. KC has seen me grow and I have seen it grow as well. I attended Altavista High School then, I did not know what was the next step in life. In our community we grow with the mentality of school not being for us. Not a single person in my family has attended collage, and most of my friends got a full time job after highschool. Not to mention, my mother did not graduate from middle school. My single mother did not have the money to put me through school so I had to look for a different way. I had not yet became a resident so FASFA and students loans were not applicable to me. More on, Universities consider me an international student, this means tuition is three times as high and I am not eligible for most of their in school scholarships. Even when you try to break an ever repeating cycle, many obstacles will drag you down.

I was able to receive some small scholarships and ended up attending a small private college, Donnelly College. They offered me a good deal and I was able to afford it by working two jobs. Two years later I was getting ready to receive my associates degree. Once again, I found myself lost not knowing what step to take next.

Luckily, one of my advisors from high school was able to talk to the president from Avila University, another private school. They accommodated into their software engineering program. At this point is where I got serious about school. Being set to graduate brought hope to my life. I became a straight A student. I enjoyed working on my assignments, programing specially. I was amazed at what lines of code could create. I remember thinking to myself at one point, "when writing code you are only limited to your imagination". Fast forward two years later, here I am, ready to receive my diploma in Software Engineering. For the third time, I felt lost. I do not know what step to take next. No one who is close to me can advise me on this issue since I am the only one to make it this far in my circle. I am eager to see what the future holds for me.
